Why GPM

Built to feel product-grade from first login to ongoing operations.

Trusted access posture

GPM starts with a pinned trusted domain, then extends into signed directories and local cache fallback so teams can keep moving with a predictable trust model.

Mesh transport with graceful fallback

Direct mesh connectivity stays the preferred path, while managed relay routing preserves continuity when peers are unreachable or network conditions change.

Role-aware operations

Client onboarding stays self-serve, operator activation stays permissioned, and every transition remains visible to the people who need to approve it.

Wallet-native sign in

The portal is designed around wallet-authenticated sessions, making identity handoff clear without adding unnecessary credential sprawl.

One language across surfaces

Desktop and web share the same product story, which keeps onboarding, support, and governance workflows easier to understand at a glance.

Operator-grade visibility

Application status, approvals, and lifecycle changes are modeled as explicit states instead of hidden background steps.

How it works

A simple 3-step flow from wallet auth to operator approval.

01

Authenticate with a wallet

Users begin in the portal with wallet-based sign in, establishing a verified session before they request access to network functions.

02

Register the client

The client is registered with a chosen path profile and bootstrap information so the mesh can connect through the right operating posture.

03

Approve the operator

Operator onboarding is reviewed and approved separately, giving admins a clean control point for elevated access and server-side responsibilities.

Trust and governance

Clear controls for teams that need confidence, not just connectivity.

Approval trails

Operator applications and state changes are designed to be reviewable, helping teams understand who approved what and when.

Least-privilege role separation

Client and operator workflows are intentionally separated so elevated access is granted only when the right conditions are met.

Secure bootstrap strategy

The first trusted contact is explicit, while downstream discovery relies on signed, durable directories and controlled fallback behavior.

Operational clarity

Status messages, portal states, and product language stay consistent so support teams and operators can act quickly with fewer assumptions.
